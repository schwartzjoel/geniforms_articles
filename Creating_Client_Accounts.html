Creating Client Accounts

When you are upgraded to a Vendor Your Company plan, your organization account is effectively upgraded to what we call a Master level account.


The Master account is the top-level account that you can use as your testing sandbox and a central area for demos and evaluations.

If you intend on selling ready-made app packages to your clients, then your Master account is where you can create such content.
You can have up to 10 users on this Master account free of charge, for demonstration and testing purposes only.


One key function that you gain from a Master account is the ability to create separate organization accounts for each of your clients.

These client accounts are completely standalone - they each have their own set of Users, Screens, Data Sources, Tasks, etc.


Client accounts are linked to your Master account and you are billed according to the number of active users in each client account.


You can also create "support users" in these client accounts that are not billed, allowing you to log directly into a client's account or app.
For details about support users, see our article on this subject.


Creating a New Client Account

  • Go to Platform&Billing>Clients
  • Click the "Add New" button in the right-hand corner.



  • On the Create Client page. Fill out your client's Basic and Regional Settings, then hit the "Create Client" button.
    You should see the message "Organisation xxx was created successfully".



Since you have created a new client account, remember that it is empty - it has no screens, data sources, users, etc.

You now need to set up the client's users, screens, etc, while being in the client account's context.


What is Client Account Context?


When you create a new Client account, the system also switches your operating context into that client account.

What this means is that the system will act as if you are logged in to that account as an administrator.

You should also see a little box at the top of the page which has the client account name.

This shows you what your current account context is, if you click on it then you will be taken to the Clients page.



You can switch your account context between clients and your master account through the Clients page.
