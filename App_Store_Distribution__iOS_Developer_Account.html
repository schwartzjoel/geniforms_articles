App Store Distribution: iOS Developer Account

For us to publish your branded app under your company brand in the Apple App Store, you will need to sign up for an Organisational iOS Developer Account. 

Developer Accounts cost USD 99 per year. It is your responsibility to pay for and maintain your Developer Account subscription. 


Get started here: 


https://developer.apple.com/programs/enroll/.

 

Create a new Apple ID

We strongly encourage you to create a NEW Apple ID during this process, since you will need to provide us with your Developer Account’s Apple ID login details in order for us to perform and manage the app submission. 

Make sure you enrol in the IOS Developer Program and pay the enrolment fees. Once you have created the account and enrolled into the IOS Developer Program successfully, please send us the Apple ID login details and we will take care of setting up the required certificates and other aspects.

What you need to Enroll in the Apple Developer Program

Organizations enrolling in the Apple Developer Program will need:

  • Legal Entity Status
    To enroll in an Apple Developer Program, your organization must be a legal entity so that it can enter into contracts with Apple. We do not accept DBAs, Fictitious Businesses, Trade names, or branches. The legal entity name will appear as the “seller” for apps you distribute on the App Store and Mac App Store.Example: Seller: ABC Company, Inc.

  • A D-U-N-S Number
    Your organization must have a D-U-N-S Number so that Apple can verify your organization’s identity and legal entity status. These unique nine-digit numbers are assigned by Dun & Bradstreet and are widely used as standard business identifiers. You can check to see if your organization already has a D-U-N-S Number and request one if necessary. They are free in most jurisdictions.

  • Legal Binding Authority
    As the person enrolling your organization in the Apple Developer Program, you must have the legal authority to bind your organization to legal agreements. You must be the organization’s owner/founder, executive team member, senior project lead, or have legal authority granted to you by a senior employee.


Requesting a D-U-N-S number (if you don't already have one)


The D-U-N-S Number is a unique nine-digit number that identifies business entities on a location-specific basis. Assigned and maintained by Dun & Bradstreet (D&B), the D-U-N-S Number is widely used as a standard business identifier.

D&B may have already assigned your organization a free D-U-N-S Number. Before enrolling, use the D-U-N-S Number lookup tool to see if you have one. If your company is not listed, you'll have the option to submit your information to Dun & Bradstreet for a free D-U-N-S Number. You can also go directly to http://www.dnb.com/duns-number.html

You will be asked for the following information when requesting a D-U-N-S Number.

  • Reason for requesting a D-U-N-S number. Choose the "I'm an Apple Developer" answer.
  • Legal entity name
  • Headquarters address
  • Mailing address
  • Your work contact information


As part of their verification process, a D&B representative may contact you directly for more information (e.g., type of business, number of employees). Please have your business registration documents ready.


Receiving and Managing Your D-U-N-S Number

After requesting a D-U-N-S Number, please allow up to 5 business days to receive your number from D&B. Expediting your D-U-N-S Number creation process will not shorten this waiting period. If your application has taken longer than two weeks to process, please email D&B.

Once you receive your D-U-N-S Number, please allow up to 2 business days for Apple to receive your information from D&B. After this period, you may enroll as a company/organization in the Apple Developer Program.

To update your D&B profile, please email D&B. It takes up to 14 business days for D&B to provide updated information to Apple.

 

Enrolling in the Apple Developer Program


  • Go to https://developer.apple.com/programs/enroll/
  • Sign in using your new Apple ID
  • At the bottom of the page, click on the Start Your Enrollment button

  • Select “Company/Organisation” enrollment type and follow the prompts. 

  • Purchase the $99 Developer Account Membership



  • Once you have created the account and enrolled in the IOS Developer Program successfully, please send us the Apple ID login details and we will take care of setting up the required certificates and other aspects




Renewing Your Apple Developer Membership


Your Apple Developer Account expires annually, 1 year from the date that you first purchased or last renewed it. 

The cost is USD 99 per year. 

Apple will send you reminder notifications to ensure your app is not removed from the App Store. If the account expires before you renew it your iPhone & iPad apps will be removed from Apple's App Store until you renew the account.


Here’s how to renew your account, regardless of if the account has expired yet or not.

  • Go to https://developer.apple.com/programs/ and click “Account” to log in.
    If you are unsure of your username and password, check your Your Company Branding Center for details of the Apple ID being used.
  • Click "Renew Membership" next to the iOS Developer Program Membership alert at the top.
  • Click “Continue.”
  • You will then be prompted to fill in your credit card information to pay for an additional year of service with Apple.
  • If prompted, we recommend you choose the "Auto-renewal" option, which means that Apple will automatically renew your membership each year - as long as your credit card details remain valid.
