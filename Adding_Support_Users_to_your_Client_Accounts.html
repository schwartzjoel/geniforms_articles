Adding Support Users to your Client Accounts

When setting up, testing and supporting your clients, you will usually need a user login to run the app under that specific client's account.
This is needed so you can test the Screens you've built and provide support to your clients when they have issues.

To meet this need, your team can create "support users" in your client accounts, free of charge.
"Support users" are strictly for testing and support purposes - these accounts cannot be used as a production user by your client.
We have a "soft limit" policy of 1 free support user per client - so you can have more support users in a client account when necessary - but please note that we actively monitor for abuse of this facility.

Creating a support user is easy - simply ensure you are logged into your client's account context and navigate to Organization & Users -> Users & Groups.


Then hit the "Add User" button to create a new user in your client's account.
Support users are identified by our billing system as having the same email address as a team member registered in your master account.
So you MUST ensure that your support users are created with an email address that matches a master account user's email exactly.
Failure to use a matching master email address will result in your support user being picked up for billing by our system.

Our platform automatically creates a support user whenever you create a new client account.
The email address of the support user created is copied from the currently logged in master account user that creates the client.

Notes


  • We recommend you seek to have a single support user account in your client accounts, using a generic email address rather than a personal email.
    i.e. create your support users with email of "support@yourcompany.com" rather than "john@yourcompany.com"
  • While not required, we recommend that you set different passwords on each support user you create.
    This is because the app distinguishes existing users already installed on the device via their password by default.
    Consider using a standard support user password approach which differs say by the client's name - clientABC vs clientXYZ
  • Every new support user that you create is an independent user in it's own right.  
    There is no link or synchronisation between the test/support users and the team member's master user account.
