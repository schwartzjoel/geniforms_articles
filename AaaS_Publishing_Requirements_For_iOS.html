Privacy Policy

As part of this process, you will also need to provide a link to your company's Privacy Policy.
This must be hosted as a page on your company's website.


If you don't already have a Privacy Policy page, then you can generate a privacy policy document quite easily - just do a web search for "privacy policy generator". Here's one such site that generates a reasonably standard privacy policy:

https://app-privacy-policy-generator.firebaseapp.com

Once you have appropriate privacy policy words generated, add this as a web page to your standard company website.
The result should be that your policy can be accessed via a website link - e.g. www.mycompany.com/privacy-policy.
Make a note of this link, since you will need to provide it to us as part of your app publishing process.

If you're not sure about how to add a Privacy Policy page to your company website, then ask the IT person/provider that maintains your website for help.

App Review Details

Apple will review your app every time it is submitted for updates.  

This means that you MUST make available a demo user account (within your main Your Company account) that Apple reviewers can use to log into your app and verify it works.  Generally, reviewers don’t do much in the way of testing, but you must ensure that the demo account is working at all times, with a few sample Screens installed on that user account for test purposes.  

The Screens in the demo account should be consistent with your stated app - e.g. don't have medical Forms in your demo account if your app is being targeted at real estate!


Enabling NFC support, iOS only supports reading of tags after a video showing the functionality has been provided when they review apps.

Display Images/Screenshots

Apple requires that all App Store listings contain a minimum of 2 screenshots showing your app in action.

We require you to provide us with these 2 (or more) screenshots, which MUST be accessible from the Demo Account Email that you specified in the App Review Details section above.

For more information about screenshot sizes please see this article 


You can use any iOS device to capture a screenshot as follows:

  • Press and hold the menu button (the main button below the screen).
  • While the menu button is held down, press the power/lock button (the switch on the top edge of the device).
  • Your screen will flash white for a second and make a camera shutter noise to indicate that the screenshot has been taken. 
You can also hold the lock button and press the menu button, or try to press the two buttons simultaneously.

A screenshot is automatically saved in the default Photos app under an album called "Saved Photos" or "Camera Roll" depending on your device.  
From here you can email one or more screenshots or other pictures, by clicking the menu icon (a rectangle with an arrow coming out of it, in the bottom left corner of the screen). Clicking the menu icon from a single picture will email the image.  
If you click the menu button from an index page, you can select multiple pictures to email.

Please note the following:
  • Images MUST be in PNG file format
  • Copyright images will be rejected by Apple (e.g. picture of Mickey Mouse) as well as images that feature likenesses to Apple or other mobile devices

Important Tips for a Smooth iOS Submission
  • Ensure all screenshots provided are relevant to the app you are publishing and is consistent with what your App Store listing text says about the app.
    i.e. do not include shots of medical forms if your app is being listed as a real estate app!

  • Apple reviewers seek to ensure that apps use in-app purchase (since Apple gets 30% of the price) whenever there is a suggestion of money being charged for app use.
    Obviously, in B2B apps like those created with Your Company, in-app purchase is entirely unsuitable since all fees are charged to the business entity and never the employees/contractors (who would be forced to pay for their own personal credit cards if an in-app purchase was in place).
    Apple struggles to understand this - the reviewers seem to be highly incentivized to force in-app purchase use.
    They also tend to think in consumer app terms and do not "get" B2B apps.
    Thus when you create App Store listing text, you should use language that clearly explains that business entities are the customer (and charged as such) and that the app is a complement to your existing cloud service, rather than the app being the sum total of the solution.
    NOTE: We do not support in-app purchase and have no plans to do so in the future.

  • Do not include direct signup links in any App Store listing text.  Apple does not accept this.

  • Do not include links to websites or anywhere else in the text. Apple usually sees this as advertising and rejects it.

  • Generally, less is more - i.e. the less you say in your listing text, the less chance Apple finds something to reject :)
